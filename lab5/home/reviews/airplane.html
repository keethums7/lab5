Airplane - Gold Standard for Parody

Airplane is a parody of the disaster films of the 1960's and 70's. Ted Striker, the main protagonist, is a war veteran with an (understandable) fear of flying. When his girlfriend, Elaine, breaks up with him before her upcoming flight - she works as a flight attendant - Ted boards the flight to try and keep his relationship intact and win Elaine back.

When a bout of food poisoning threatens to take down the entire plane, it's up to Ted and everyone else spared from the tainted food to safely land the plane. Easier said than done.

Score: 8/10 - Airplane is...silly. It is ridiculous and an easy laugh, and you should watch it simply because it's stupid and enjoyable. There will be no lesson or takeaway, and you won't be smarter for having watched it, but gosh darn it it's lovely. With Leslie Nielsen as one of the pilots of the plane, and a whole slew of other terrific comedic talent, this is a classic that is surely worth the watch.
